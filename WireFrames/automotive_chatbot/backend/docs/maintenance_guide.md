# 🔧 Singapore Vehicle Maintenance Guide

## 🚗 Tire Maintenance & Replacement

### How to Change a Tire - Complete Guide

**When to Change Tires:**
- Flat tire or puncture
- Tread depth below 1.6mm
- Sidewall damage
- Age over 6 years
- Uneven wear patterns

**Tools Required:**
- Jack and jack stands
- Lug wrench
- Spare tire (properly inflated)
- Wheel chocks
- Gloves
- Flashlight (if dark)

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Safety First**
   - Park on level, stable ground
   - Turn on hazard lights
   - Apply parking brake
   - Place wheel chocks behind opposite wheels

2. **Preparation**
   - Remove spare tire and tools from trunk
   - Loosen lug nuts (don't remove completely yet)
   - Locate proper jack point under vehicle frame

3. **Lifting the Vehicle**
   - Position jack securely under designated lift point
   - Raise vehicle until flat tire is 6 inches off ground
   - Never put any part of body under lifted vehicle

4. **Removing the Flat Tire**
   - Fully remove loosened lug nuts
   - Keep lug nuts in safe place
   - Pull tire straight toward you to remove from wheel hub

5. **Installing Spare Tire**
   - Align spare tire with wheel bolts
   - Push spare tire onto wheel hub
   - Replace lug nuts and hand-tighten

6. **Lowering Vehicle**
   - Lower vehicle until spare tire touches ground but still supports some weight
   - Fully tighten lug nuts in star/cross pattern
   - Lower vehicle completely and remove jack

7. **Final Check**
   - Ensure all lug nuts are properly tightened
   - Check spare tire pressure
   - Store flat tire and tools in trunk

**⚠️ Important Safety Notes:**
- Spare tires are temporary - drive slowly (max 80 km/h)
- Get permanent tire replacement as soon as possible
- If on highway, call for professional roadside assistance

### Tire Replacement Frequency

**Recommended Schedule:**
- **Every 40,000-80,000 km** depending on driving conditions
- **Every 6 years** regardless of mileage (rubber degradation)
- **Check monthly** for proper inflation pressure

**Signs You Need New Tires:**
- Tread depth below 1.6mm (use coin test)
- Cracks in sidewall
- Bulges or blisters
- Excessive vibration while driving
- Uneven wear patterns

## 🛠️ Engine Maintenance

### Oil Change Schedule
- **Conventional Oil:** Every 5,000-7,500 km
- **Synthetic Oil:** Every 10,000-15,000 km
- **Severe Conditions:** Every 3,000-5,000 km

### Air Filter Replacement
- **Every 15,000-20,000 km** or annually
- Check monthly in dusty conditions
- Clean air filter improves fuel efficiency

### Spark Plug Replacement
- **Standard Plugs:** Every 30,000-50,000 km
- **Platinum/Iridium:** Every 80,000-100,000 km

## 🔋 Electrical System

### Battery Maintenance
- **Lifespan:** 3-5 years in Singapore climate
- **Monthly Check:** Terminal corrosion and voltage
- **Signs of Failure:** Slow engine cranking, dim lights

### Alternator & Starter
- Regular voltage testing during service
- Listen for unusual noises during startup

## 🧽 Fluids & Filters

### Brake Fluid
- **Replacement:** Every 2 years or 40,000 km
- **Check:** Color should be clear, not dark

### Coolant System
- **Flush:** Every 2-3 years or 50,000 km
- **Check Level:** Monthly when engine is cool
- **Singapore Climate:** Extra attention due to heat

### Transmission Fluid
- **Automatic:** Every 60,000-100,000 km
- **Manual:** Every 50,000-80,000 km

### Power Steering Fluid
- Check level monthly
- Flush every 50,000 km or 3 years

## 🚙 Body & Interior

### Air Conditioning
- **Service:** Every 6 months in Singapore
- **Filter Replacement:** Every 15,000 km
- **Refrigerant Check:** Annually

### Cabin Air Filter
- **Replacement:** Every 15,000-25,000 km
- **Singapore:** More frequent due to air pollution

## 🛣️ Suspension & Steering

### Shock Absorbers/Struts
- **Inspection:** Every 20,000 km
- **Replacement:** Every 80,000-120,000 km

### Wheel Alignment
- **Check:** Every 20,000 km or if steering pulls
- **After:** Hitting potholes or curbs

## 📅 Singapore-Specific Maintenance Tips

### Climate Considerations
- **High Humidity:** More frequent AC service
- **Tropical Heat:** Engine cooling system attention
- **Frequent Rain:** Brake and tire inspections

### Urban Driving Conditions
- **Stop-and-Go Traffic:** More frequent oil changes
- **Short Trips:** Battery and engine concerns
- **Parking:** Paint protection from sun damage

### LTA Regulations
- Must pass inspection before COE renewal
- Keep maintenance records for insurance claims
- Follow manufacturer recommended service intervals

## 🔍 Monthly Inspection Checklist

**Fluids:**
- ✅ Engine oil level and color
- ✅ Coolant level (when cold)
- ✅ Brake fluid level
- ✅ Power steering fluid
- ✅ Windshield washer fluid

**Tires:**
- ✅ Tire pressure (including spare)
- ✅ Tread depth and wear patterns
- ✅ Sidewall cracks or bulges

**Lights & Electrical:**
- ✅ Headlights, taillights, indicators
- ✅ Brake lights and hazard lights
- ✅ Battery terminals (clean and tight)

**Engine Bay:**
- ✅ Belt condition and tension
- ✅ Hose connections and leaks
- ✅ Air filter cleanliness

## 🆘 Emergency Maintenance

### What to Do If:

**Engine Overheats:**
1. Pull over safely immediately
2. Turn off engine and wait 30 minutes
3. Check coolant level (when cool)
4. Call for assistance if problem persists

**Brake Problems:**
1. Pump brakes gently if they feel soft
2. Use parking brake gradually if needed
3. Pull over safely and call for help
4. Never continue driving with brake issues

**Strange Noises:**
- **Grinding:** Brakes or bearings - stop driving
- **Squealing:** Belts or brake pads - check soon
- **Knocking:** Engine problems - stop immediately
